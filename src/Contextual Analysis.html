A. Historical Context

Time Period

  • The book called “the boat” was published in 2008 where a time was global migration, and displacement were pressing socio-political issues.
  • The stories is also within the collection span multiple periods, from the Vietnam war ( as seen in the boat ) to contemporary conflicts and social struggle ( as seen in Tehran calling and Cartagena ).

Major Events or Cultural Movements

  • Mai, a Vietnamese refugee, and her experiences on a boat with two hundred others, including the young Quyen and her son Truong, as they seek a better life, facing illness, starvation, and despair, ultimately ending in tragedy with Truong's death.

B. Cultural Context

Norms and Values Reflected in the Text

  • Family honor and individual decisions: the characters are action are often motivated by a desire to protect their love ones. ( love and honor, meeting elisse ).
  • Violence and power structure: the danger of wars, crime and oppression are central to stories like Cartagena and Tehran calling.

Examples from the Text

  • Survival and family loyalty - "The boat was a mess. People were sick, vomiting into plastic bags. The heat was unbearable. There was a baby, crying. Its mother was trying to soothe it, but she was weak, her eyes sunken. They had been at sea for days, with little food or water. The men were arguing, their voices hoarse. They spoke of pirates, of storms, of death. No one spoke of rescue."

C. Reception

Initial Reception

  • Upon its 2008 release, Nam Le's short story collection "The Boat" received generally positive and enthusiastic reviews, with many praising its unique voice and scope, and even calling it a debut of a major writer.

Evolution of Interpretations

  • "The Boat" by Nam Le is a collection of seven short stories exploring themes of displacement, alienation, and the human condition, culminating in the title story about Vietnamese boat people fleeing the new Communist state.
